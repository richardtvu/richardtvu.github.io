Values and Beliefs

My hero is Dr. Jordan Peterson.

Listening to Peterson and acting upon his ideas has lead me to consciously choose to believe that:

  • Life is suffering.
  • Suffering presents an opportunity to have meaning in life by bearing the heaviest burden that I can bear.
  • Happiness, as a neurochemical state, is ephemeral, and it is unwise to seek it out as a permanent state.
  • By contrast, meaning is something achievable on a moment-by-moment basis, as long as I choose to--in the words of Alcoholic Anonymous:
    • Accept what is out of my control with serenity.
    • Act upon what I can control with courage.
    • Cultivate the wisdom of differentiating between the two.
  • Unless, I develop and use my capacity for truth, then I stand no chance of living up to the above. That is why, it is important for me to "Tell the truth ... or at least don't lie."
  • Telling the truth is a discipline and it must be exercised each day.
  • Discipline is not a matter of sprinting, but a matter of building microhabits that allow me to reduce the cognitive burden of working on what I consider meaningful.
  • By developing this discipline, I gain a key to fulfillment, to paraphrase George Leonard.
